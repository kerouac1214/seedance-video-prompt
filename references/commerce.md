# Commerce and Ad Prompt Patterns

Use this file for product videos, e-commerce, UGC ads, TikTok Shop, product showcases, brand films, unboxing, before/after, feature explainers, premium/luxury visuals, food and beverage, and conversion-focused scripts.

## Start With the Selling Problem

Choose the ad pattern from the product's bottleneck, not from visual style alone.

| Selling problem | Better pattern |
|---|---|
| Product looks cheap or unknown | Retail shelf / premium context / social proof scene |
| User doubts quality or delivery | Inspection, packing, stress test, shipping preparation |
| Benefit is invisible | Macro, cutaway, time-lapse, before/after, demonstration |
| Needs authority | Expert desk, lab, interview, documentary proof style |
| Needs urgency | News-style update, countdown, limited stock scene, live-shopping energy |
| Needs premium feel | Macro material, slow highlight sweep, editorial object handling |
| Needs immersion | POV unboxing, first-person use, action-camera demo |
| Needs UGC volume | Casual phone testimonial, mirror shot, desk/car/kitchen routine |
| Needs exact sequence | Grid storyboard or timestamped shot plan |

## Product Prompt Recipe

```text
[duration/aspect/platform].
@图片1中的产品作为唯一产品，保持外观、颜色、比例、包装和细节一致。
Hook: [visual problem or curiosity in first 1-2s].
Demo: [how product is handled/used; visible benefit].
Proof: [macro detail, comparison, context, user reaction, process].
Payoff: [hero shot, result, call-to-action if requested].
Camera: [phone handheld / macro studio / retail shelf sweep / POV / turntable].
Audio: [packaging, click, pour, fabric, voiceover, ambient].
Constraints: no extra logos, no unreadable tiny text, no distorted product shape.
```

For brands, only use brand names/logos when the user owns the brand, provides the asset, or clearly asks for work on their own brand. Otherwise rewrite as an original unbranded product.

## High-Performing Ad Families

### 1. Product Showcase

Best for beauty, sneakers, tech, watches, packaged goods.

Pattern:

```text
Macro hero object on a controlled surface. Start with texture or silhouette, then reveal full product. Use highlight sweeps, condensation, dust motes, turntable rotation, focus pull, and tactile sound. End on stable hero frame.
```

### 2. UGC Authentic

Best for social proof, low-cost tests, creator-like ads.

Pattern:

```text
Phone-shot, imperfect framing, casual environment, product enters naturally, subject demonstrates one believable use, small real-world interruption or adjustment, quick honest reaction. Keep lighting practical and audio captured.
```

### 3. Before / After

Best for cleaning, skincare, organization, footwear, food refresh, visual transformations.

Pattern:

```text
Open on an unflattering but believable "before"; show one decisive intervention; reveal result with changed light, texture, cleanliness, glow, steam, shine, or order. Avoid impossible claims.
```

### 4. Unboxing / ASMR

Best for gadgets, accessories, cosmetics, small luxury goods.

Pattern:

```text
Near-POV hands, packaging texture, tissue/foam/label details, slow reveal, tactile audio: peel, click, slide, fabric, glass, cardboard. Keep product centered after reveal.
```

### 5. Problem to Solution

Best for pain-point hooks.

Pattern:

```text
First 1-2s shows the annoying problem; next beat introduces the product; middle shows product solving it; final beat shows relief/result. Use simple cause-effect visuals.
```

### 6. Lifestyle / Brand Feel

Best for premium positioning and brand mood.

Pattern:

```text
Place product inside a believable ritual: morning desk, gym bag, rainy commute, kitchen counter, travel kit. Product is used, not just displayed. Environment and user behavior sell identity.
```

### 7. Feature Explainer

Best for tech, tools, design features, mechanisms.

Pattern:

```text
Use clean close-ups, labels only if requested, cross-section/cutaway/metaphor overlays, and cause-effect demonstration. Each feature should produce a visible change.
```

### 8. Social Trend

Best for reels, comparison hooks, green-screen, this-or-that, quick reaction formats.

Pattern:

```text
Start with a recognizable social-native framing: mirror, car seat, desk, green-screen pointer, split-screen, outfit transition, or quick comparison. Keep cuts fast and casual.
```

### 9. Luxury / Premium

Best for jewelry, fragrance, watches, leather, premium packaging.

Pattern:

```text
Slow macro, controlled reflections, dark or neutral surface, edge light, precise hand movement, minimal text, quiet sound, material specificity: brushed metal, frosted glass, embossed paper, stitched leather.
```

### 10. Food and Beverage

Best for appetite appeal.

Pattern:

```text
Show physics: pour, steam, melt, crackle, bubbles, crumbs, oil sheen, condensation, knife cut, sauce stretch. Use close audio and natural light.
```

## E-Commerce Playbook

Use these when the user wants conversion, not just beauty.

| Play | Use when | Prompt ingredients |
|---|---|---|
| Retail shelf trust | White-label or unknown product needs credibility | product on shelf, comparable products, store lighting, shopper POV |
| Quality check | Returns/fragility worry | worker checks product, test process, careful packaging, shipping prep |
| Macro mechanism | Benefit is hard to see | close-up, cutaway, particles/liquid/fiber/skin/surface behavior |
| Expert proof | Needs authority | calm expert, desk/lab setting, one clear claim, visible demonstration |
| Growth/result visualization | Slow results | time-lapse, staged before/after, honest non-medical wording |
| Premium detail | Looks cheap | macro probe feel, material texture, editorial light, slow handling |
| POV try/use | User needs to imagine ownership | first-person open/use/wear/play, natural mistakes |
| Creator testimonial | Needs native social feel | selfie framing, one pain point, one demo, one reaction |
| Grid storyboard | Needs many beats | 9/16/25 grid reference, explicit reading order |

## TikTok / UGC Realism

For social commerce, polish can reduce trust. Use:

- A believable creator situation: just arrived, rushed morning, after workout, desk break, store aisle, bathroom mirror, kitchen counter.
- One product action per beat.
- Imperfect but readable face/hands/product.
- Practical light, room tone, packaging sounds.
- Concise speech: one hook, one proof, one payoff.

Avoid:

- Overly cinematic camera moves unless the product category demands it.
- Perfect model behavior.
- Unrealistic claims, especially medical, body transformation, finance, or guaranteed results.

## Copy and On-Screen Text

Seedance can add text, but small text often becomes unreliable. If text matters:

- Use short lines.
- Put only one message per beat.
- Ask for large, centered, high-contrast text.
- Avoid legal fine print or dense ingredient lists.
- For brand lockups, use provided image assets as references when possible.

## Commerce Checklist

- Does the first 1-2s have a visual hook?
- Is the product identity locked to `@图片1` or a precise description?
- Is the benefit visible, not just claimed?
- Does the ad pattern match the selling problem?
- Are claims compliant and believable?
- Is the final frame useful: product hero, result, CTA, or continuation?

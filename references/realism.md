# Realism and Anti-AI Patterns

Use this file when the user wants realistic, documentary, phone-shot, TikTok/Reels UGC, DV/VHS, surveillance, webcam, or "not AI-looking" video prompts.

## Core Principle

First decide the footage identity:

```text
Who captured this, on what device, in what year, for what reason?
```

Once the footage identity is clear, camera flaws, framing, audio, light, and behavior become coherent. Realism usually comes from controlled imperfection, not extra polish.

## Seven-Block Prompt

For realistic prompts, structure the output as:

1. Main subject: visible traits, clothes, hair, skin texture, physical state, consistency lock.
2. Location: time, region, environment elements, light behavior, exclusions.
3. Visual style: realism level and source identity.
4. Camera style: device defects and operator behavior.
5. Timeline: 5-7 short beats with action, environment response, one imperfection per beat.
6. Audio: on-location sounds only, unless the user asks for music.
7. Goal: one sentence describing what the footage should feel like.

## Device Packs

### Modern Smartphone Vertical

Use for TikTok, Reels, selfie, shop demo, street encounter, vlog.

```text
9:16 vertical phone video, slight wide-angle front/back camera distortion, handheld single-hand framing, mild electronic stabilization with residual walking bob, occasional autofocus hesitation, small exposure shifts when moving between shadow and light, over-sharpened phone HDR texture, imperfect off-center composition. No tripod stability, no cinema dolly movement, no professional studio lighting.
```

### Early-2000s Consumer DV

Use for nostalgic neighborhoods, family tape, casual travel, old friend footage.

```text
Consumer DV camera look, faded colors, soft contrast, mild digital compression artifacts, sensor noise in shadows, harsh highlight clipping, handheld wobble, uneven zoom speed, autofocus breathing, exposure pumping when crossing sunlight, abrupt start/end as if recorded by a person. No modern color grading, no gimbal stability, no ultra-clean 4K sharpness.
```

### VHS / 90s Home Video

Use for older memory, retro party, analog uncanny scenes.

```text
VHS home camcorder texture, low resolution, visible tape noise, scanline softness, color bleed, slight edge distortion, date/time stamp if useful, heavy handheld movement, awkward zoom, white balance drift. No digital crispness, no HDR, no smooth cinematic motion.
```

### 8mm / Super 8

Use for dream memory, wedding nostalgia, art film.

```text
Super 8 film feel, warm color shift, visible film grain, gate weave, dust and hair scratches, vignetting, 18fps slight stutter, short hard-cut shots, soft overexposed highlights. Minimal sync sound unless intentionally added.
```

### GoPro / Action Camera

Use for first-person sports, travel, outdoor product use, immersive demo.

```text
Chest/head-mounted action camera POV, ultra-wide fisheye distortion, edge stretching, high saturation, lens flare or tiny lens marks, body movement transmitted to frame, sudden head turns, strong but imperfect electronic stabilization. No third-person cinematic tracking, no perfect composition.
```

### CCTV / Surveillance

Use for objective record, mystery, shop, warehouse, security angle.

```text
Fixed high-angle CCTV view, low frame rate, low resolution, compressed shadows, timestamp in corner, static camera or slow preset pan, people unaware of camera, no sound unless the scene requires silent footage. No photographer presence, no deliberate composition, no zoom tracking.
```

### Professional Documentary

Use for credible but polished human stories.

```text
Handheld documentary camera, natural light, restrained color, subject observed patiently, occasional focus lag, micro-wobble from operator breathing, long takes, subject sometimes glances at camera then looks away. No staged commercial lighting, no dramatic music-driven editing, no glossy ad polish.
```

## Anti-AI Imperfections

Select one or two per prompt, or one per timeline beat for realism-heavy work:

- Autofocus searches between subject and foreground.
- Framing drifts because the operator follows late.
- Exposure changes when clouds, windows, or shade shift.
- A hand, strap, shelf edge, passerby, steam, fabric, or reflection briefly obstructs part of the frame.
- Subject starts an action, hesitates, then adjusts naturally.
- Hair, clothing, packaging, paper, condensation, dust, or steam reacts to movement.
- A sound interrupts speech and the subject reacts.
- The ending feels recorded: phone lowers, subject exits frame, frame holds too long, or cut to black.

Avoid stacking too many flaws. One believable imperfection beats a catalog of glitches.

## Human Realism

Do not write emotions as final states only. Use progression:

```text
0-1s: trigger.
1-3s: body reacts first.
3-5s: face catches up, still partly controlled.
```

Use visible micro-details:

- Eyes search, blink rate changes, gaze avoids/returns.
- Mouth tightens, breath shortens, jaw or throat moves.
- Fingers tap, grip, release, adjust clothing or object.
- Clothing has wrinkles, edge lift, moisture, lint, or use marks when appropriate.
- Skin has natural texture, uneven tone, subtle redness, small shine, and non-plastic highlights.

## Environment Realism

Translate mood into observable phenomena.

| Mood | Observable details |
|---|---|
| Warm | late sunlight through leaves, cup steam, soft household noise, fabric moving in a small breeze |
| Nostalgic | faded color, older street furniture, visible utility wires, date stamp, outdated clothing details |
| Lonely | negative space, distant traffic, fluorescent spill, reflections in glass, slow movement in background |
| Uneasy | flickering light, long hallway shadow, slight handheld tremor, interrupted ambient sound |
| Busy | layered foreground/midground/background movement, overlapping voices, practical lights, small work gestures |

## Realism Checklist

- Is the footage identity clear?
- Do camera flaws match the device and era?
- Are background elements alive but not distracting?
- Are human actions process-based rather than pose-based?
- Does the audio sound captured, not designed, unless the user asked for polish?
- Are negative constraints compatible with the style?
